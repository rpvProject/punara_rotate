## 4. PROBLEM — `#problem`

**Label (mono):** WHY BRANDS LOSE REPEAT REVENUE

**Headline:** You can see every ad click. You can't see who's coming back.

**Intro:** You've industrialised acquisition — attribution, creative testing, daily ROAS. Meanwhile the customers you already paid for, the only ones with margin left in them, sit in a list nobody models. Six failures, one pattern:

**Six one-liners (list, each with its cost mechanism):**

1. **Fragmented data.** Shopify, Razorpay, Shiprocket, Klaviyo, Interakt each hold a piece of the customer; nobody joins them — so one buyer appears as three profiles and every number downstream inherits the error.
2. **Guesswork segmentation.** Demographic hunches send the same 10% code to a full-price loyalist and a lapsed discount-hunter — margin lost in both directions at once.
3. **Dead lifecycle flows.** The welcome series someone built in 2023 still runs; replenishment, winback, and COD confirmation — the moments with money in them — have nothing running at all.
4. **No experimentation.** Changes ship on opinion, so wins can't be repeated and losses can't be found; revenue moves and nobody can say why.
5. **Report theatre.** Monthly decks count sends and opens. Activity gets reported, outcomes don't, and the deck's real job is to renew the deck.
6. **One-blast-fits-all.** Every send to everyone borrows revenue from next week, trains buyers to wait for codes, and burns the deliverability you'll later pay to rebuild.

**Sub-headline:** What the leaks look like in rupees

**Three leak archetypes (tiles, mono numerals):**

- **COD returns-to-origin.**
  A ₹40cr brand with half its orders on COD and a 20% RTO rate has ~₹4cr of dispatched orders coming back every year. Freight, reverse logistics, and written-off stock on those parcels typically burn **₹1.2–1.5cr/yr** — a line most P&Ls never itemise.

- **Silent churn.**
  At a 22% repeat rate, 78 of every 100 customers you paid ~₹400 each to acquire never come back — no complaint, no unsubscribe, no signal. One point of repeat rate at this scale is worth roughly **₹44L/yr** (15,000 orders/mo × 1 pt × ₹2,200 AOV × 1.1 repeat-order multiplier × 12).

- **Discount-only buyers.**
  When a third of "repeat" revenue only converts with a code, that isn't loyalty — it's margin leaving in instalments. On ₹12cr of repeat revenue at 25% average code depth, the discount-dependent slice costs about **₹1cr/yr** in contribution.

**Closing line:** None of this is a creativity problem. It's a measurement problem — and measurement problems have engineering answers.

> **Design intent:** Bone reading section, generous whitespace; the three archetype tiles use Ember for the leak figures — Ember is semantic (a leak), never decorative.

## 11. FAQ — `#faq`

**Label (mono):** THE QUESTIONS WE ACTUALLY HEAR

**Headline:** Asked and answered, before the call.

**Q1 — We already have an agency. Why do we need you?**
Keep them. Your agency executes campaigns; nobody is pricing which customers will return or auditing what those campaigns are worth. We're the layer above: we hand your agency a scored segment and a forecast, and we measure what they ship — most agencies do better work against our Ledger. If your agency resists being measured, that finding alone is worth the audit.

**Q2 — Klaviyo already shows me retention data.**
Klaviyo shows what happened inside Klaviyo. It doesn't see your RTO rate, your COD failures, your Razorpay payment retries, your Shiprocket delivery times, or which acquisition cohort is quietly dying — and it will never flag that its own flows are underperforming. We read Klaviyo plus the rest of your order graph and score the whole system. Ask Klaviyo for your CIQ; it doesn't have one.

**Q3 — Why is the audit paid when others offer free ones?**
A free audit costs its author nothing to be wrong, and its conclusion was written before your data arrived: "hire us." The Decode is priced — ₹1,95,000 / $2,900 — so it has to stand alone: ten scores, a leak map in rupees, a 90-day plan you could hand to any team, including someone else's. And 100% of the fee credits against a retainer signed within 60 days, so working with us makes the audit cost nothing extra.

**Q4 — How fast do we see results?**
The Loop Ledger is ranked by payback speed, and the fastest lines are usually leak fixes — RTO interception, failed-payment recovery — which bank inside the first quarter. Repeat-rate compounding is slower: typical engagement arithmetic covers the fee at about +1.4 points of repeat rate, and we never forecast beyond +6 points in year one, whatever a spreadsheet permits. First scores in 14 days. First re-score at day 30. First rebaseline at day 90.

**Q5 — Do you run our campaigns, or just advise?**
Both exist, priced separately. Ignite (₹1,50,000/mo / $2,500) is advisory: we decide, your team executes. Momentum and above (from ₹3,00,000/mo / $5,000), we operate inside your stack — Klaviyo, Interakt, your BSP — ourselves. What we never do is execution without the method: "just run our emails" is an agency engagement, and we're not an agency.

**Q6 — What data access do you need?**
Read access to orders and customers in week one: Shopify, Razorpay, Shiprocket, and your messaging platforms; anything without a connector arrives as a CSV export. It's a condition of starting, not a request — you cannot score data you cannot see. Eighteen months of order history is enough. Messy is fine; the Signal Score exists to price the mess. Missing is not.

**Q7 — What does it cost?**
All of it is on this page. The Decode: ₹1,95,000 / $2,900, one-time, 100% creditable. Retainers: ₹1,50,000–₹9,00,000 per month ($2,500–$14,000), flat, 3-month minimum, no success fees — attribution in Indian D2C is too disputable to price honestly, so accountability lives in a public monthly CIQ instead of a rev-share you'd spend every review disputing. Annual prepay takes 10% off. That is the only discount that exists.

**Q8 — Do you work with brands outside India?**
Yes — US, UK, and Australian Shopify brands, priced in USD, delivered remotely. The method was built on the hardest version of the problem — COD economics, RTO, WhatsApp-first customers — and it travels: the scores, the models, and the Loop are identical; only the channel mix changes. If your repeat cycle is under 12 months and your data lives in Shopify, geography isn't the constraint.
